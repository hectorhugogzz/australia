## 3. Housing & Neighborhoods

*   **Top Recommended Suburbs:** Based on family-friendliness, commute to the Arts Precinct, and good schools.
    1.  **Camberwell:** Affluent, established, direct tram access.
    2.  **Glen Iris:** Family-oriented, direct tram access.
    3.  **Kew:** Leafy, affluent, direct tram access.
    4.  **Northcote:** Trendy, multicultural, good transport links.
    5.  **Malvern:** Upscale, good transport links.
*   **Bayside Suburbs (Closer, more urban):**
    *   **Port Melbourne, Albert Park, Middle Park:** Excellent direct tram access (~10-15 mins), but housing is more often apartments/townhouses.
    *   **South Yarra vs. St Kilda:**
        *   **South Yarra:** Safer, more prestigious, excellent schools. The recommended choice.
        *   **St Kilda:** More vibrant Latin community, but significantly higher crime rates.
*   **Rental Estimates (3-Bedroom House, Weekly):**
    *   **Glen Waverley:** ~$650
    *   **Northcote:** ~$745 - $880
    *   **Camberwell / Kew / Malvern:** ~$850 - $1,050+
    *   **Toorak / Albert Park:** ~$1,200 - $1,300+

## 5. Moving & Furnishing

*   **Strategy:** **Sell most bulky items in Mexico.** The cost of shipping (~$12,400+ USD for a container), customs, and potential biosecurity issues make it more practical to buy new and second-hand in Melbourne.
*   **Items to Pack:** Laptops, computers, monitors, clothes, and essential personal items.
*   **Estimated Furnishing Costs (Melbourne):**
    *   **Full 3-4 BR House (New):** AUD $16,000 - $32,000+
    *   **Key Tech Items to Purchase:**
        *   Hector's Office (Desk/Chair): ~AUD $800 - $1,500
        *   Home Monitor/Dock: ~AUD $350 - $700
        *   Alex's Gaming Desk/Chair: ~AUD $300 - $800

## 6. Transportation

*   **Public Transport:** Melbourne has an excellent, integrated system (train, tram, bus) using the **Myki card**. The Arts Precinct is highly accessible via tram from most recommended suburbs.
*   **Driving:**
    *   You can use your Mexican license for the **first 6 months**.
    *   After 6 months, you **must convert to a Victorian license**, which will require passing knowledge, hazard, and practical driving tests.
*   **Car Ownership:**
    *   **Used Family Car:** Budget AUD $10,000 - $30,000 for purchase.
    *   **Annual Running Costs:** ~AUD $5,700+ (Registration: ~$930, Insurance: ~$3,500, Maintenance: ~$1,250+).

---

## 7. Banking & Initial Steps

*   **Banking:**
    *   **Recommendation:** Open an account online with **Commonwealth Bank (CommBank) or Westpac** up to 3 months before arrival.
    *   **Action:** Upon arrival, visit a branch to verify ID and activate the account.
*   **Key First-Week Tasks:**
    1.  Activate bank account.
    2.  Get Australian SIM cards.
    3.  Apply for Tax File Numbers (TFNs) online.
    4.  Buy Myki cards for transport.
    5.  Begin attending rental inspections.

## 8. Travel to Melbourne

*   **Best Time to Book Flights:** Between **July 2025 and January 2026** for a July 2026 departure.
*   **Estimated Cost (Family of 4, Economy):** **$6,000 - $6,800 USD**.
*   **Best Time to Fly:** July is off-peak season, which should result in more favorable pricing compared to the summer months.

# Transportation in Melbourne

## Public Transportation System
*   **Integrated System:** Utilizes trains, trams, and buses with the Myki smart card.
*   **Myki Card:** A reloadable, contactless card for fares. "Touch on" at the start and "touch off" at the end of your journey. Can be topped up at stations, retailers, online, or by phone.
*   **Trams:** Extensive network. A **Free Tram Zone** exists in the city center where no Myki is needed.
*   **Trains:** Metro trains connect suburbs to the CBD via the City Loop.
*   **Buses:** Cover areas not serviced by trains or trams.
*   **Fares (as of Jan 2024):**
    *   2-hour full fare (Zones 1+2): AUD $5.30
    *   Daily full fare: AUD $10.60
    *   Weekend/Public Holiday daily cap: AUD $7.20
*   **Services:** A Night Network runs on weekends. The PTV App is available for journey planning.

## Driving & Car Ownership
*   **Driving in Melbourne for New Arrivals:**
    *   **Using Overseas License:** You can drive on a valid overseas license for up to 6 months after arrival. If the license is not in English, you must carry an International Driving Permit (IDP) or a formal English translation.
    *   **Converting to a Victorian License:** This is mandatory after 6 months. As Mexico is a "non-recognised country," you will likely need to pass a road law knowledge test, a hazard perception test, and a practical driving test.
*   **Cost of Buying a Car in Melbourne:**
    *   **Used Family Car Purchase Price:** A reasonable budget is AUD $10,000 - $30,000.
    *   **Registration (Annual):** Approx. AUD $930.70 (includes compulsory third-party insurance).
    *   **Transfer Fees (One-off):** AUD $44.90 + motor vehicle duty (based on market value).
    *   **Insurance (Annual Comprehensive):** Approx. AUD $3,502 for a "Family" profile.
    *   **Ongoing Maintenance (Annual):** Budget AUD $1,250 - $1,750+ for routine servicing, tyres, and unexpected repairs.

## Scooter and E-Scooter Rules (Victoria)
*   **Age Limit:** Riders must be **16 or older**.
*   **Allowed Surfaces:**
    *   Roads with a speed limit of up to 60 km/h.
    *   Shared-use paths (e.g., bike paths).
    *   Bicycle lanes.
*   **Prohibited Surfaces:** **Footpaths are strictly prohibited.**
*   **Speed Limit:** A maximum speed of **20 km/h** must be observed.
*   **Safety Rules:**
    *   **Helmets are mandatory.**
    *   Mobile phone use while riding is illegal.
    *   Only one person per scooter (no passengers or animals).
    *   Riders must obey all road rules (traffic lights, signs, etc.).
    *   Riding under the influence of alcohol or drugs is illegal and carries heavy penalties.
    *   Lights must be used at night or in low-light conditions.

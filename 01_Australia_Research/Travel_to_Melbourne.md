# Travel to Melbourne from Mexico (Relocation)

> **Updated (Jul 2026):** Move date shifted to **late January / early February 2027**. This is priced as a **one-way relocation** for a family of 4 (not round-trip). Australian summer / tail of peak season — see notes below.

*   **Route Reality:**
    *   No nonstop flights **Mexico City (MEX) → Melbourne (MEL)**; expect **1+ stops**, ~21–22h total travel time.
    *   **Puebla (PUE)** has no practical international departure — position to **MEX** by private shuttle/van (~2 hrs) with luggage: **~$100–$200 USD**.

*   **Estimated Flight Costs (One-Way, Economy, Family of 4):**
    *   **Per person one-way:** ~$1,008–$1,160 USD (cheapest/restrictive) up to **$1,300–$1,900 USD** (realistic mid-range).
    *   **Family of 4 (flights only):** **~$4,200 – $7,600 USD**.
    *   **+ Positioning (PUE→MEX):** ~$100–$200 USD.
    *   **+ Extra/overweight relocation baggage:** ~$400–$1,000 USD (one-way move = max bags, ~$100–200 per extra bag).
    *   **TOTAL PLANNING ESTIMATE:** **~$4,700 – $8,800 USD (≈ $7,200 – $13,500 AUD).**
    *   **Realistic target:** **~$5,500 – $7,500 USD (≈ $8,400 – $11,500 AUD)** by flying early February and booking Oct–Nov 2026.

*   **Best Time to Book:**
    *   **2–4 months in advance** for this route → book **October–November 2026**, which aligns with the expected visa grant (Nov–Dec 2026). Consider flexible/refundable fares if booking before the grant.
    *   Cheapest to book/fly **mid-week (Wed/Thu)**.

*   **Best Time to Fly (Season):**
    *   **February is one of the cheaper months** on this route (post-New-Year dip). **Early February prices better than late January**, which is still the tail of the Australian summer-holiday peak.
    *   ⭐ **Cost lever:** Shifting arrival from **late January → early February** is the single biggest way to reduce airfare.

*   **Note vs. old plan:** The previous July estimate (~$9,200–$10,500 AUD) benefited from off-peak winter pricing. Moving to one-way + peak-adjacent Jan/Feb makes the total **similar or slightly higher**, because one-way long-haul fares are disproportionately expensive and relocation baggage adds cost.

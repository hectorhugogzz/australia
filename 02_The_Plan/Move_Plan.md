# Month-by-Month Plan: Moving from Puebla to Melbourne

This document outlines a detailed timeline of tasks to ensure a smooth transition from October 2025 to your arrival and initial settlement in Melbourne in July 2026.

### **Phase 1: Preparation & Applications (October 2025 - March 2026)**

#### **October - December 2025 (9-7 Months Out)**
*   **[ ] Visa Process:**
    *   **Hector & Liz:** Begin preparing for your IELTS exams. Use practice tools like ELSA Speak.
    *   **Hector:** Coordinate with Bundle of Rays (BoR) to formally begin the Sponsorship and Nomination stages for the Skills in Demand (Subclass 482) visa.
    *   **Hector:** Confirm with BoR if a formal skills assessment from the Australian Computer Society (ACS) is required. If so, begin gathering all necessary documents (qualifications, detailed work experience letters, etc.). This can be a lengthy process.
*   **[ ] Schooling (Natalia - VCASS):**
    *   **Natalia:** Begin selecting and preparing her three contrasting monologues (one classical, two contemporary) for the VCASS video audition.
    *   Monitor the VCASS website for the official opening of 2027 entry applications.
*   **[ ] Finances:**
    *   Start tracking current household expenses in detail to build a baseline for your Melbourne budget.
    *   Begin setting aside funds for the move (visa costs, flights, initial rental bond, etc.).

### **Phase 3: Arrival & Settlement (July 2026 onwards)**

##### **Weekly Breakdown: July 2026**
*   **Week 1: Immediate Essentials (Arrival Week)**
    *   **[ ] Banking:** Go to your chosen bank branch (CommBank or Westpac) to verify your identity, activate your accounts, and collect your debit cards.
    *   **[ ] Connectivity:** Purchase Australian SIM cards for the entire family.
    *   **[ ] Government:** Apply for your Tax File Numbers (TFNs) online via the ATO website.
    *   **[ ] Transport:** Buy Myki cards for using Melbourne's public transport system.
    *   **[ ] Orientation:** Settle into your temporary accommodation and begin exploring the immediate neighborhood on foot.
*   **Week 2: Housing & Schooling**
    *   **[ ] Housing:** Dedicate this week to finding a long-term rental. Attend as many inspections as possible in your target suburbs.
    *   **[ ] Housing:** Prepare and submit rental applications. Have digital copies of all necessary documents ready (passports, visas, proof of funds/employment).
    *   **[ ] Schooling (Alex):** Contact your chosen secondary school to formally enroll Alex for the start of Term 3 (mid-July).
*   **Week 3: Securing a Home**
    *   **[ ] Housing:** Hopefully, you receive a rental offer this week. Review and sign the lease agreement.
    *   **[ ] Housing:** Pay the rental bond and the first month's rent.
    *   **[ ] Utilities:** Once the lease is signed, arrange for electricity, gas, and internet to be connected at your new home.
*   **Week 4: Initial Furnishing**
    *   **[ ] Settling In:** Move into your new long-term home.
    *   **[ ] Furnishing:** Begin purchasing essential items: mattresses, a refrigerator, a washing machine, and a temporary table and chairs.
    *   **[ ] Furnishing:** Order the specific office and gaming equipment needed:
        *   Hector's Office: Elevable desk, ergonomic chair.
        *   Home Computer: 27" monitor, docking station.
        *   Alex's Room: Gaming desk and chair.

##### **August 2026 Onwards: Finalizing Setup**
*   **[ ] Health:** Register for Medicare (if eligible) or confirm your private health insurance is active.
*   **[ ] Schooling (Natalia):** Attend the on-site VCASS audition if invited.
*   **[ ] Transportation:**
    *   Decide whether to purchase a car. If so, begin looking at used car dealerships.
    *   Begin the process of converting your Mexican driver's licenses to Victorian licenses (book knowledge/hazard/practical tests).
*   **[ ] Furnishing:** Continue furnishing your home.
*   **[ ] Community:** Explore extracurricular activities for the children (ice skating for Natalia, Taekwondo for Alex).
*   **[ ] Finances:** Ensure your TFNs have been provided to your employer (Hector) and bank to avoid excess tax.
